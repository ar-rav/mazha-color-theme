Brand color theme proposal — 5-color palette with gradients, roles, and rationale

Overview

Five carefully tuned hues form a cohesive, nature-forward system. Analogous harmony (adjacent hues 75°–140°) creates unity, while temperature balance (cool greens + warm browns) adds depth. Following the 60–30–10 rule keeps interfaces calm and ordered while gradients-to-white create airy, botanical surfaces.

Accent A • Forest Primary background • Sage Accent B • Olive Secondary surfaces • Taupe Anchor / Text • Espresso

Why 5 Colors?

Color theory principle: The 60-30-10 rule suggests limiting palettes to avoid visual chaos. With gradients, we effectively have 25 visual elements.

Color Theory Justification

Why five colors?

Five hues keep the system clear and harmonious with the 60–30–10 guideline. Gradients-to-white add softness without adding extra hues.

  • Two greens for freshness and depth
  • Two browns for warmth and grounding
  • Olive as the natural bridge between families

Color roles at a glance

  1. Sage Green (H120 S20 L75) — Light anchor
    • Soft, sophisticated lightness with low saturation
    • Neutral green base that plays well with warm and cool accents
    • Ideal for backgrounds and subtle gradients to white
  2. Forest Green (H140 S50 L25) — Deep accent
    • Blue-leaning green adds richness and visual tension with Sage
    • Higher saturation preserves color identity at a darker value
    • Great for primary actions and emphasis
  3. Warm Taupe (H30 S25 L55) — Mid neutral
    • Comforting orange-brown tilt brings warmth
    • Balanced mid-light value for cards and panels
    • Low saturation keeps UI elements from competing
  4. Espresso Brown (H25 S40 L20) — Darkest anchor
    • Warm depth for headings, borders, and high-contrast text
    • Sufficient saturation avoids muddiness at low lightness
    • Pairs powerfully with Forest for complementary tension
  5. Olive Green (H75 S30 L45) — Bridge
    • Sits naturally between green and brown families
    • Reads as green or earth tone depending on context
    • Unifies the palette with an analogous arc

Harmony in practice

  • Temperature: Cool (Forest) → Neutral (Sage/Olive) → Warm (Taupe/Espresso)
  • Values: Light (Sage) → Mid (Taupe/Olive) → Dark (Forest) → Darkest (Espresso)
  • Saturation: Higher at darker values to keep colors vivid; refined at lighter values for calm surfaces
